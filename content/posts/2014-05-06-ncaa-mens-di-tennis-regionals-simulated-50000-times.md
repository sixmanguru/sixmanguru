</div><p>This is posted on my college tennis website so aptly named, <a href="http://texascollegetennis.com/">texascollegetennis.com</a>. I decided to post it here as well.. why not, right?</p>
<p>I’m sitting in the middle of exams and term projects looking for ways to relax. What better way than to run a Monte Carlo Simulation of each of the men’s regionals, based on my year-end ratings?</p>
<p>So I ran each regional 50,000 times and you can see the results. One of the more intriguing of course is the Nashville Regional, where I could not really account for the home court (and probably outdoor) advantage the Vanderbilt will have over Columbia. That probably sways things a little, I am guessing in the range of 5-15%.</p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5;">The first number is how many times each team won the entire regional. The second is their probability of coming out of the regional.</span></p>
<p><strong>BEST FIRST ROUND MATCH-UPS:</strong> The Vanderbilt-Virginia Tech match-up looks good, as do the Oklahoma State-Michigan, Memphis-Drake, South Florida-Florida State,  Northwestern-Mississippi, Wake Forest-Louisville, Boise State-USD and Auburn-Harvard. There are a few more, but that’s my quick take.</p>
